Why This Matters

+

Did you know that 87% of gamers are bullied online? Toxicity is now the leading reason players quit gaming. What was once designed as a space for creativity, collaboration, and play has, for many, become a source of stress, exclusion, and harm. The need to ensure psychological safety in gaming environments has never been more urgent—for developers, studios, and communities alike.

+ +
+

Session Overview

+

This session is designed for game developers, esports organizers, community managers, trust-and-safety professionals, educators, and policymakers who shape the future of digital play. Together, we will explore how behavioral science and AI-driven technologies can reduce toxicity, enhance player well-being, and strengthen trust across gaming ecosystems. Protecting emotional safety online doesn’t just promote mental health—it preserves the freedom to play, compete, and create without fear of abuse or silencing.

+

Inside the Session

+

Drawing on recent validation studies and large-scale player data, I will use live gameplay examples and AI demos to show how psychological factors (empathy gaps, emotional regulation, dark triad traits) and environmental drivers (anonymity, toxic reward loops, competitive modeling) contribute to harmful behaviors. We’ll also examine how real-time behavioral analytics and AI-guided interventions can transform community culture from reactive moderation to proactive prevention.
